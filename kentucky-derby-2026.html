The Canadian Connection

🍁
Mark Casse
Hall of Fame Trainer · London, Ontario

Canada's most celebrated trainer had his Silent Tactic scratched on April 29 with a bruised foot — ending his quest for a first-ever Kentucky Derby win. Casse, 65, called the decision easy: "We're always going to try to do what we feel is best for the horse."

A 17-time Sovereign Award winner as Canada's outstanding trainer, Casse is inducted into both the Canadian Horse Racing Hall of Fame (2016) and the National Museum of Racing Hall of Fame (2020). His remarkable résumé includes seven Canadian Triple Crown races — the King's Plate with Lexie Lou (2014), Wonder Gadot (2018), and Paramount Prince (2023) — plus two-thirds of the American Triple Crown in 2019 with War of Will (Preakness) and Sir Winston (Belmont).

The plan is now the Preakness Stakes at Laurel Park on May 16. Don't count Canada out of the Triple Crown picture.

🐎
Silent Tactic
Scratched — Targeting Preakness

One of the most intriguing longshot stories of the 2026 Road to the Derby, Silent Tactic had finished first or second in all six career starts. His runner-up finish to Renegade in the Arkansas Derby showed genuine Derby-calibre talent.

A bruised foot — described by Casse as similar to a "fingernail separation" — forced the painful scratch just days before the race, breaking the heart of jockey Cristian Torres, who had been set to make his Kentucky Derby debut.

Next stop: Preakness Stakes, May 16, Laurel Park. The Canadian flag may yet fly over a Triple Crown race in 2026.

🏆
Woodbine & Canada's Racing Heritage
A Nation of Horse Racing Passion

Canada has a rich thoroughbred tradition anchored at Woodbine Racetrack in Toronto, home to the prestigious King's Plate — North America's oldest continuously run stakes race, dating to 1860. Canadian-bred and Canadian-connected horses have long made their mark on the international stage, and the 2026 Derby class carries that legacy proudly.

Mark Casse's long dominance of the Sovereign Award — winning it 17 times, an unprecedented achievement — reflects Canadian thoroughbred racing's remarkable depth of talent and ambition.

As Derby fans in Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal and across the country tune in on May 2nd, Canada's racing community is already looking ahead to the Preakness — and dreaming of roses of a different colour.

Fan Note: While Canada won't have a runner in the starting gate on Saturday, the Maple Leaf runs deep through the fabric of this Derby. Mark Casse remains one of the most respected figures in North American racing, and when Silent Tactic lines up at Laurel Park on May 16, the whole country will be watching. Go Canada! 🍁
